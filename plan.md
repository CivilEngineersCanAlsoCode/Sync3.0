# Deep Research Execution Plan: 100 PM Roles (B2C/AI/Design-First with B2B Optional, Compensation & Stability First)

## Overview

This is a **step‑by‑step execution guide** for a human researcher or an AI agent.  
Follow each phase sequentially. Document all findings in a structured format (spreadsheet or database) for later scoring and ranking.

**Core Priorities (Updated):**

- **Compensation is the top factor** – target CTC 40–50 LPA for remote India roles, or US/EU roles with equivalent high pay ($150k+). You are willing to relocate for the right opportunity.
- **Stability & Growth** is the second most important factor – this includes company longevity, financial health, funding momentum, domain growth potential, and the opportunity for ownership/career acceleration.
- **Work mode** still matters but carries less weight because you are open to relocation.
- **Industry preference**: B2C is preferred over B2B, but you are **not entirely against B2B** if the role offers exceptional compensation, stability, and growth. The scoring will reflect this nuance.

All other preferences (role type, visa needs, notice period, etc.) remain as originally stated.

## Phase 2: Enrich Company Data

**Goal:** For each company in the master list, collect specific data points needed for scoring. Use a combination of public sources and estimation rules. Be extra thorough for compensation and stability indicators.

### Step 2.1 – Remote policy & work mode

- Check company website → "Careers" page, "About us", or "Culture" page. Look for keywords: "remote‑first", "distributed team", "hybrid", "location: anywhere".
- LinkedIn "Jobs" section for current openings – note location/remote tag.
- Glassdoor "Company" page – reviews often mention remote work.
- If unclear: search Google "<Company Name> remote work policy 2025".

**Data to record:**  
`Remote Policy` = Fully remote / Hybrid (specify cities if possible) / In‑office (city)

### Step 2.2 – CTC / compensation range (HIGHEST PRIORITY – be exhaustive)

- **Primary sources:**
  - Current job posts (look for salary ranges; some US companies post them, Indian companies sometimes do).
  - Levels.fyi – search by company and role (Product Manager, Senior Product Manager).
  - Glassdoor "Salaries" section.
  - AmbitionBox (for India).
  - Blind app – sometimes people share offers.
- **Secondary / estimation:**
  - Use benchmark tables based on stage and location:
    - Indian startups: Seed‑A: 20‑30 LPA, Series B‑C: 25‑40 LPA, Series D+ / MNC: 35‑65 LPA.
    - US startups: $100k‑$140k, US big tech: $140k‑$220k.
  - Adjust based on funding (more funding → usually higher pay).
  - Check news for "highest paying startups" lists.
  - If multiple data points, record the range.
  - If no data, mark "Unknown" and use default later.

**Data to record:**  
`CTC Min (INR LPA)`, `CTC Max (INR LPA)` – convert USD to INR (~85).  
`Currency` (INR/USD)  
`Source of data` (e.g., Levels.fyi, job post, estimated from stage)

### Step 2.3 – Role types available & growth potential

- Visit company careers page, search for "Product Manager", "Senior Product Manager", "Chief of Staff", "Founders Office".
- Also note if they are actively hiring for PM roles now or recently (last 3 months).
- **For growth potential:** look for signals like "first PM hire", "building new product from scratch", "core product team", "high-impact role" in job descriptions.

**Data to record:**  
`Role Types` = PM / SPM / CoS / etc.  
`Hiring Status` = Active / Recent / Not hiring but likely  
`Role Impact Signal` = High / Medium / Low (based on job description keywords)

### Step 2.4 – Company stage, funding, and stability indicators (detailed)

- Crunchbase: look for "Last Funding Date", "Last Funding Type", "Total Funding Amount".
- Also look for estimated revenue or growth metrics (from news, LinkedIn, or business press).
- Stage classification:
  - Seed / Angel
  - Series A, B, C, D+
  - Public
  - Bootstrapped
- Profitability (if known): profitable / not profitable / approaching profitability.
- Funding health: recent round size, investor reputation (top-tier VCs like Sequoia, Accel, etc. indicate stability).
- If available, employee count growth (LinkedIn "Employees" history).

**Data to record:**  
`Stage`  
`Total Funding (USD)`  
`Latest Round Amount (USD)`  
`Latest Round Date`  
`Investors` (list top 2‑3)  
`Profitability` (Yes/No/Unknown)  
`Revenue Estimate` (if available, e.g., $10M ARR)  
`Employee Growth` (YoY %, if available)

### Step 2.5 – Work‑life balance reputation (lower priority now)

- Glassdoor: average rating and look for keywords in reviews (e.g., "good work‑life balance", "long hours").
- AmbitionBox (India): similar.
- Blind app (for tech companies).
- If no data, leave blank.

**Data to record:**  
`WLB Rating` (1‑5, with notes)  
`Source`

### Step 2.6 – Visa sponsorship (for US/EU roles)

- Check job posts for phrases: "must be eligible to work without sponsorship", "we offer visa sponsorship".
- Check company policy pages or H1B database (for US).
- If unknown, assume no.

**Data to record:**  
`Visa Sponsorship` = Yes / No / Unknown

### Step 2.7 – Domain growth score (1‑10)

- Based on Phase 0 domain score. If the company operates in one of the identified emerging domains, assign that domain's score.
- If domain not in Phase 0 list, research quickly:
  - Search "<domain> market growth 2026" or "<domain> VC investment trend".
  - If no strong evidence, assign a middle score (5).

**Data to record:**  
`Domain Growth Score`

### Step 2.8 – Industry type (B2C/B2B/Both)

- Based on product focus. If a company has both, mark "Both". This will be used in the industry alignment scoring.

**Data to record:**  
`Industry Type` = B2C / B2B / Both

### Step 3.2 – Default handling for missing data (applied systematically)

- **Compensation**: Use stage/location defaults from scoring guide.
- **Work mode**: Assume hybrid in HQ city.
- **WLB**: default = 5.
- **Stability & Growth**:
  - Financial stability: if stage known, use that as base (e.g., Series B = 5). If stage unknown, assume Series B = 5.
  - Domain growth: if no Phase 0 match, default = 5.
  - Role impact: if no info, default = 5.
  - Combine as per formula.
- **Industry alignment**: if industry type unknown, assume B2C and non‑emerging = 8.

### Step 3.3 – Calculate total score

`Total = (CompScore * 0.4) + (StabilityGrowthScore * 0.3) + (WorkModeScore * 0.15) + (IndustryScore * 0.10) + (WLBScore * 0.05)`

Multiply each factor score (0‑10) by weight, sum to get 0‑100.

### Step 3.4 – Ranking & tie‑breaking

Sort by total score descending. If tie, prioritize:

1. Higher Compensation Score
2. Higher Stability & Growth Score
3. Higher Industry Alignment Score (favor B2C)
4. Higher Work Mode Score

### Step 3.5 – Select top 100

From ranked list, pick top 100 for final output.

### Step 4.2 – For each platform, gather:
