**How does this fit company roadmap?**  
“This fits the roadmap because it strengthens the core value delivery of Sprinklr Insights. Sprinklr is an enterprise platform, and the biggest challenge in enterprise platforms is adoption and time-to-value. Use Case Hub improves onboarding speed, reduces services dependency, and makes the product easier to use across roles. That directly supports the company’s growth goals because it makes implementations faster, reduces friction for new customers, and increases adoption across existing accounts. It also improves the product story for sales and marketing because the platform looks ‘industry-ready’ instead of ‘heavy and complex.’”

**Is this a feature or platform bet?**  
“This is a platform bet. It is not just one feature like a new dashboard. It changes how the product is adopted and scaled. We productized what was earlier done through consultants: topic creation, taxonomy setup, dashboard creation, and persona environments. That means it impacts multiple parts of the product lifecycle—onboarding, reporting, adoption, action workflows, and role-based access. Platform bets are about repeatability and scale, and that’s exactly what this did.”

**What is 3-year vision?**  
“My 3-year vision is to make Insights truly self-serve and outcome-driven. A customer should be able to enter their brand, choose industry and goals, and the system should recommend the best listening setup, dashboards, and action workflows automatically. Over time, it becomes an ‘Insights Operating System’—it not only shows what is happening, but it tells you what matters, why it matters, and what actions to take. Also, persona experiences become more intelligent: leadership gets executive summaries and alerts, managers get operational playbooks, and analysts get deep exploration tools. The goal is that customers go from setup to decision-grade reporting in hours, not weeks.”

**What is v2?**  
“In v2, I would improve two major areas. First, recommendations: based on industry and early data patterns, the product should suggest which dashboards, themes, and alerts the customer should enable, instead of just offering a list. Second, quality loops: we should learn from customer edits—like what keywords they remove or add, which templates they use, what dashboards they customize—and use that to improve the default setup over time. I would also expand coverage gradually: more industries, more use cases, and better multilingual support, because language and region differences are real in global enterprise customers.”

**Would you spin this into a standalone product?**  
“I would not spin it out as a completely separate product in the short term because its biggest value is inside the Sprinklr platform—it improves adoption and usage of the Insights module and ties into data ingestion, reporting, and governance. But conceptually, the ‘Use Case Hub’ idea can be a standalone layer: a guided marketplace of ready-to-use intelligence workflows and templates. If the company wanted to create a separate packaged offering, it could be positioned as a ‘fast-start insights solution’ for mid-market or for faster deployments. Still, strategically, I think it is strongest as a core platform capability that increases adoption, retention, and scalability across the existing suite.”
