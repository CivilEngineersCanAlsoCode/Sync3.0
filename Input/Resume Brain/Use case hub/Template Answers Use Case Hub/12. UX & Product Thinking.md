**How did you reduce cognitive load?**  
“I reduced cognitive load by removing two kinds of overload: setup overload and UI overload. Setup overload was coming from too many manual steps—creating topics, adding keywords, creating themes, and then building dashboards. I reduced that by automating the starting configuration using AI-assisted entity creation and by giving ready templates. UI overload was coming from the fact that Sprinklr is a large enterprise suite and not every user needs every module. So Persona App was key: analysts, managers, and leadership see different experiences with different permissions and simplified navigation. In simple terms, I removed unnecessary choices and repeated work, and I made the product feel smaller and clearer for each role.”

**Why your chosen user journey?**  
“I chose the ‘Listen → Learn → Act’ journey because that matches how customers actually think about insights work. First they need to listen and collect the right data, then they need to learn through reporting and dashboards, and finally they need to act—otherwise insights are useless. Earlier, the product was mostly strong in listening and reporting but weak in making action easy, and it also required too much setup effort. This journey made the experience guided and predictable. It also helped in demos because it clearly explains the value in a structured way rather than showing a complex UI with many menus.”

**What usability metrics did you track?**  
“The main usability metrics I tracked were around speed and self-serve success. For example, how quickly a customer can create a topic and reach a working dashboard, and how much consultant help is needed. I also looked at adoption indicators like how many users actually activate templates, how many dashboards are created from templates, and whether action features like alerts and scheduled reports are configured. For Persona App, I tracked persona deployment time and whether leadership users actually engage with scheduled reports and alerts, because leadership is the hardest group to adopt complex enterprise tools.”

**How did you validate design?**  
“I validated the design by using a mix of internal and customer-facing validation. Internally, I involved consultants early because they are the ones who know what works across industries and they know where customers struggle. I also validated the flow with account teams because they see how customers understand the product in demos and onboarding. For the product experience, we tested whether users can complete the core journey—create data capture, see dashboards, and set actions—without needing deep training. The goal was: ‘Can a customer succeed with fewer steps and fewer decisions?’”

**What did users initially struggle with?**  
“Initially, users struggled in two areas. First, they didn’t always trust the automated setup, especially AI-generated keywords, because enterprise users care a lot about accuracy. So we made the suggestions transparent and editable so they can review and adjust. Second, leadership users struggled with navigation and finding the ‘one view’ they need, because the standard enterprise UI is too broad. That’s why Persona App mattered — it gave them a simplified experience focused on dashboards, alerts, and scheduled reports instead of full platform complexity.”
