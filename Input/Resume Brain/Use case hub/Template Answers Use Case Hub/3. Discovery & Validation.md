**How did you validate the problem?**  
“I validated the problem by looking at repeated patterns across many Sprinklr Insights clients and across internal teams that support them. I spoke with consultants and account teams who onboard customers and asked them what takes the most time and where clients struggle. I also reviewed multiple client setups and saw that the same steps were repeated again and again: creating topics manually, defining themes manually, and building dashboards manually for common use cases. I also observed that onboarding timelines were long mainly because of this configuration work, not because of engineering limitations. That gave me confidence that the biggest bottleneck was operational friction, not missing product capability.”

**What assumptions were wrong?**  
“One assumption that was wrong early was that customers want full customization from day one. In reality, most customers want a strong default setup that works fast, and only later they want customization. Another wrong assumption was that leadership users will eventually learn the full enterprise UI. Most of them won’t. They need a simplified experience built for their role. Also, I initially thought dashboard creation was the main pain, but I realized configuration before dashboards—topics and themes—was equally painful and was the real root cause of slow onboarding.”

**What user research did you do?**  
“I did research in three ways. First, I collected qualitative inputs from internal teams—consultants, support, and account managers—because they directly see customer friction during onboarding. Second, I studied real customer configurations and dashboards across industries to understand what is common vs what is truly custom. Third, I did lightweight validation with a few users and consultants by showing the concept of a guided ‘Listen → Learn → Act’ flow and checking if it matches how they think about their work. The goal of research was to find what can be standardized safely across industries and what must remain editable.”

**What surprised you?**  
“What surprised me was how much of the setup is repetitive across customers. Even though industries differ, the core use cases are very similar—brand monitoring, crisis monitoring, sentiment tracking, campaign tracking, product mentions, executive mentions, and so on. Another surprise was that users did not just want better dashboards; they wanted the full loop—insights plus actions like alerts and scheduled reporting—because otherwise insights stayed inside the tool and didn’t reach decision makers.”

**Why this solution vs alternatives?**  
“I considered a few alternatives. One was to simply improve documentation and training, but that still keeps the product complex and consultant-driven. Another was to only build dashboard templates, but that doesn’t solve the earlier steps like topic and theme setup. Another option was to build everything fully AI-generated and fully automatic, but that carries risk of incorrect configuration and loss of trust in enterprise settings. I chose a balanced solution: automation for speed, templates for best practices, and editable controls for accuracy and flexibility. That’s why Use Case Hub combined AI-assisted setup, reusable templates, and a guided flow.”

**What evidence gave you conviction?**  
“My conviction came from clear operational evidence: onboarding was taking around 4+ weeks largely due to manual configuration, and persona setup was taking around 5 hours per persona. I also had evidence from looking at many existing client dashboards—there was a strong pattern and reusable structure across industries. That meant we could standardize a lot without losing value. Finally, the internal teams were strongly aligned that if we reduce dependency on consultants and make it self-serve, we can onboard faster, drive adoption, and scale the product without scaling services headcount.”
