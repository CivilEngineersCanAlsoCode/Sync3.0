## 14. Product Strategy & Future Vision

• How does this fit company roadmap?  
• Is this a feature or platform bet?  
• What is 3-year vision?  
• What is v2?  
• Would you spin this into standalone product?

**How does this fit company roadmap?**  
This fits the roadmap as a shift from “monitoring and dashboards” to “operational intelligence and automation,” which is where enterprise value and willingness-to-pay increase sharply. It upgrades the platform from being a passive reporting layer to being a decision layer that helps enterprises detect issues early, explain root causes, and coordinate action. Strategically, it also strengthens the platform narrative around AI-driven outcomes: not just insights, but faster resolution, lower escalation risk, and workflow integration across teams.

**Is this a feature or platform bet?**  
It’s a platform bet, because the hardest and most valuable parts—signal unification, taxonomy, clustering, prioritization, governance, routing—become reusable primitives across multiple use cases. Once you build a reliable “issue intelligence engine,” you can apply it to other ecosystems beyond driver support: customer support, retail operations, supply chain disruptions, product quality issues, etc. If it were only a feature, it would end at summarization; as a platform, it becomes the foundation for multiple downstream workflows.

**What is 3-year vision?**  
The 3-year vision is an “Issue Operating System” for enterprises: a system that continuously senses signals across internal and external channels, detects emerging problems early, explains them with evidence, prioritizes them by business impact and risk, routes them to the correct owners, and then closes the loop by learning from outcomes. Over time, it becomes more predictive and prescriptive—moving from “what’s happening” to “what should we do next,” with traceability and governance built-in so leadership trusts it as a decision layer.

**What is v2?**  
V2 focuses on turning the system from “RCA automation” into “resolution acceleration.” That includes stronger identity resolution and multilingual support, better drift monitoring and self-tuning clustering thresholds, and richer severity scoring that separates volume from criticality. On the workflow side, V2 adds playbooks and recommendations—what actions worked previously for similar clusters—plus tighter integration into ticketing and incident management so the product can track closure, verify impact reduction, and continuously improve its routing and prioritization from feedback.

**Would you spin this into standalone product?**  
It could be a standalone product as “Support Intelligence / Ecosystem RCA” because it solves a universal enterprise problem: fragmented signals and slow root-cause convergence. However, it is often more defensible and monetizable inside a broader platform because its value increases with integrations, existing data access, and workflow embedment. Standalone can win if it integrates deeply with the customer’s ticketing and ops stack and offers faster time-to-value; platform integration wins when cross-channel data and governance are already native. The strategic choice depends on go-to-market: standalone for broader adoption, platform for deeper expansion and higher enterprise stickiness.
