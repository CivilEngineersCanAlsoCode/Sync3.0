**What was your North Star metric?**  
“My North Star for this product was **Time-to-Insight / Time-to-Value** — basically how quickly a customer can start seeing useful insights after starting onboarding. For Persona App specifically, the North Star was **Persona Deployment Time** — how quickly a customer can deploy the right role-based experience for analysts, managers, and leadership. The reason I picked these is because the biggest problem we were solving was not capability; it was speed and friction to reach value.”

**Why that metric?**  
“I chose Time-to-Insight because in enterprise SaaS, the first few weeks decide adoption. If customers don’t see value quickly, internal champions lose energy, usage drops, and renewal risk increases later. Also, if onboarding is slow and consultant-heavy, the company cannot scale implementations. So reducing time-to-insight directly impacts adoption, retention, and scalability. For Persona App, reducing persona deployment time directly reduces setup effort and helps more users in the customer org adopt the product faster.”

**Leading indicators?**  
“My leading indicators were things that show early progress before renewal or long-term retention. For example: how many clients start using the templates, how many auto-created topics and themes are activated, how many dashboards are generated from templates, and how many users set up alerts or scheduled reports. Another leading indicator was reduction in the number of consultant hours needed for standard onboarding. For Persona App, leading indicators were number of personas deployed and number of role-based users actively logging in after deployment.”

**Lagging indicators?**  
“My lagging indicators were adoption and retention outcomes. For example: weekly active usage of Insights dashboards after onboarding, continued use of alerts and scheduled reports over time, and reduction in support tickets related to setup confusion. Commercially, long-term lagging indicators would be renewal health and expansion, because faster time-to-value typically improves customer satisfaction and increases willingness to pay for advanced features like deeper backfill and advanced workflows.”

**How did you measure improvement?**  
“I measured improvement by comparing the old onboarding flow versus the new guided flow. Earlier, onboarding was consultant-led and took around 4+ weeks mainly because of manual topic creation, theme creation, dashboard building, and persona setup. With Use Case Hub, customers could start from a ready flow with AI-assisted setup and templates, so the time needed to reach working dashboards reduced significantly. For Persona App, earlier persona creation took around 5 hours; after building pre-configured personas, the same deployment took around 1 hour. These comparisons were based on real implementation timelines and effort estimates from the teams doing onboarding.”

**Exact formula for 75% / 85% claims?**  
“For the Use Case Hub claim, the formula is:  
**Time-to-Insight Improvement % = (Old Time-to-Insight – New Time-to-Insight) / Old Time-to-Insight × 100**.  
In our case, if onboarding setup to reach usable insights was about **4 weeks** earlier, and with Use Case Hub it became about **1 week** for most standard setups, then:  
(4 – 1) / 4 × 100 = **75% improvement**.

For Persona App, the formula is similar:  
**Deployment Time Reduction % = (Old Persona Setup Time – New Persona Setup Time) / Old Persona Setup Time × 100**.  
If it was about **5 hours** earlier and became about **1 hour**, then:  
(5 – 1) / 5 × 100 = **80% reduction**.

If someone asks about 85% — that number belongs more to my Walmart Insight Assistant story, where we reduced time-to-insight from around 7 days to end-of-day insights. But for this Use Case Hub project, the clean numbers are 75% and 80%.”

**Retention definition?**  
“For retention, I define it based on continued meaningful usage, not just logins. A simple practical definition is: a customer or key user persona is retained if they continue using the Insights dashboards and the action features over time — for example, they are active in a later period like week-over-week or month-over-month. For leadership personas, retention could be measured by continued engagement with scheduled reports and alerts, because they may not open dashboards daily. So retention is role-based: analysts retained by dashboard usage, managers retained by monitoring workflows, and leadership retained by alerts and scheduled reporting consumption.”
