**01 Context — What Was the Real Problem?**

Client: Government of Qatar (Prime Minister’s Office + Ministries)

Core Need:

- Monitor citizen sentiment in real time
- Detect emerging issues before escalation
- Track ministry/service performance
- Understand public dissatisfaction or resentment
- Reduce response latency

Problem at root:

Data existed across multiple silos → no unified intelligence → slow insight cycles.

Government insight lag = reputational + political risk.

---

**02 Data Architecture — What Sources Did We Unify?**

We integrated 4 major intelligence streams:

1️⃣ Media Monitoring

- Public social posts with media attachments
- AI-trained image recognition (logos, ministry visuals, executive imagery)
- Brand & entity detection

2️⃣ Social Listening

- Keyword-based monitoring across all major platforms
- Track mentions of ministries, executives, services, policies

3️⃣ Mystery Shopper / Survey Data

- Government-appointed field agents
- In-person citizen interviews
- Structured satisfaction feedback

4️⃣ Social Service (Unified Complaints System)

- Public complaints via social media
- Internal helpline tickets
- Chat messages
- Escalations

All streams unified into one centralized intelligence layer.

**03 Product Goal — Our North Star**

North Star Metric:

Time to Insight = 5–10 minutes.

Why?

- <5 min → shallow scanning, early drop-off
- > 10 min → cognitive overload, confusion
    

Goal:

Decision-grade clarity within one executive sitting.

Not dashboards for analysts.

Dashboards for ministers.
