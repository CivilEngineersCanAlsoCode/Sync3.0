## 06. AI / ML Depth (When Relevant)

If you mention AI, expect drilling.

• Why unsupervised vs supervised?  
• How was clustering validated?  
• How did you prevent hallucination?  
• What was precision vs recall trade-off?  
• What was fallback if model failed?  
• How often retrained?  
• Any bias risks?

Never say: “Data team handled it.”

---

**Why unsupervised vs supervised?**  
We used unsupervised clustering for emerging issue detection because labeled training data for novel public narratives did not exist in advance. Government contexts frequently face new policy discussions, sudden sentiment shifts, or localized events that have no historical labeled dataset. A supervised model performs well only when patterns are stable and pre-defined. For early-warning detection, the objective was anomaly surfacing and thematic grouping rather than binary classification. Therefore, unsupervised topic modeling and clustering were more appropriate for surfacing emerging conversation clusters that analysts could then interpret. Supervised models were still used for known tasks like sentiment classification and entity detection, but unsupervised logic was essential for novelty detection.

**How was clustering validated?**  
Clustering validation occurred through multi-layer evaluation. First, we performed manual sampling of cluster outputs to check topic coherence—ensuring that posts grouped together shared semantic similarity. Second, we calculated intra-cluster similarity versus inter-cluster dissimilarity scores to confirm structural separation. Third, we ran human validation sessions with Arabic-speaking consultants to confirm contextual accuracy, because language nuance can distort semantic grouping. Finally, we tracked downstream usefulness: if clusters consistently resulted in actionable escalations, that validated their relevance. Clusters that repeatedly produced noise were recalibrated or threshold-adjusted.

**How did you prevent hallucination?**  
We avoided generative free-form summarization without grounding. Instead of allowing the model to invent narrative summaries, we anchored outputs strictly to source data and extractive techniques. All insights were traceable to underlying posts or complaint entries. If summarization layers were used, they were constrained by source references and confidence thresholds. We also avoided auto-recommendation outputs without validation in early phases. Executive-facing products require deterministic outputs; hallucinated narratives would destroy trust immediately. Therefore, we designed the system to surface structured signals rather than creative language.

**What was precision vs recall trade-off?**  
For early-warning detection, we prioritized recall over precision. Missing an emerging crisis is costlier than temporarily surfacing low-priority noise. However, to prevent alert fatigue, we layered prioritization rules such as volume thresholds, velocity spikes, and sentiment intensity scoring. Precision was more important in executive alert panels where false positives could cause unnecessary escalation. Therefore, recall was optimized at ingestion level, while precision was optimized at alert presentation level. This layered approach balanced risk sensitivity with cognitive load.

**What was fallback if model failed?**  
The fallback was a human-in-the-loop oversight layer. If anomaly detection confidence dropped below threshold or sentiment confidence scores were unstable, signals were routed through consultant review before escalation tagging. We also maintained raw feed visibility so that even if clustering logic malfunctioned, underlying data remained accessible. In critical cases, manual tagging workflows could temporarily override automated classifications.

**How often retrained?**  
Sentiment and entity models were retrained periodically based on drift monitoring, especially when vocabulary shifts were detected in Arabic discourse. Retraining cadence depended on drift signals rather than fixed time intervals, but in practice occurred quarterly or during major narrative shifts. Unsupervised clustering models were recalibrated when topic fragmentation increased or coherence scores degraded. Monitoring dashboards flagged distribution drift and unusual variance spikes.

**Any bias risks?**  
Bias risks were significant in Arabic sentiment interpretation, especially across dialect variations and culturally sensitive terminology. Gendered phrasing and honorific expressions could be misclassified if models were trained on generic datasets. There was also risk of over-amplifying highly active digital segments while underrepresenting offline populations. We mitigated linguistic bias through native consultant validation loops and threshold tuning. We mitigated representation bias by incorporating survey and mystery shopper data alongside digital signals, ensuring intelligence was not purely social-media skewed.
