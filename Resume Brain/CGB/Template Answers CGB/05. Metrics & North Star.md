## 05. Metrics & North Star

Weak PMs speak impact. Strong PMs define formulas.

• What was your North Star metric?

• Leading indicators?  
• Lagging indicators?  
• How did you measure improvement?  
• Exact formula for 75% / 85% claims?  
• Retention definition?

---

**What was your North Star metric?**  
The North Star metric was Time to Insight (TTI). It was defined as the time required for an executive user to log in and reach actionable clarity on priority risks across sentiment, complaints, and media signals. In practical terms, it measured how long it took for a decision-maker to answer the question, “Is there anything that requires intervention today?” without consultant assistance. The target range was 5–10 minutes per session.

**Why that metric?**  
Because the core problem was not data availability but decision latency. Executives operate in constrained attention windows. If insight clarity takes more than 10 minutes, cognitive overload increases and usage drops. If it is under 5 minutes but shallow, signal prioritization may be inadequate. Time to Insight directly reflected the product’s ability to convert raw intelligence into decision-grade clarity. It aligned with the job-to-be-done: rapid governance awareness.

**Leading indicators?**  
Leading indicators included session duration within the optimal range, alert click-through rate, percentage of sessions that reached high-risk modules, reduction in navigation depth required to reach escalation signals, and frequency of executive logins per week. We also tracked module abandonment rates and reduction in consultant-dependent interpretation during executive meetings.

**Lagging indicators?**  
Lagging indicators included executive retention rate, contract renewal probability, reduction in complaint escalation lag time, and increased executive adoption across ministries. We also monitored sustained usage consistency over quarters, not just initial adoption spikes.

**How did you measure improvement?**  
We established baseline metrics from pre-redesign usage logs, including average session duration, navigation depth, and module engagement. Post-redesign, we compared the same cohorts across defined periods. For example, if average time to identify a priority issue previously exceeded 20 minutes and post-redesign stabilized within 7–9 minutes, that constituted measurable TTI improvement. We also compared engagement dispersion across modules; concentration of engagement in prioritized views indicated clarity improvement. Qualitative validation from executive sessions further confirmed reduced interpretation time.

**Exact formula for 75% / 85% claims?**  
For Time-to-Value reduction claims in related dashboard redesign work, the formula was:  
Time-to-Value Reduction % = (Baseline Time − Post-Redesign Time) / Baseline Time × 100.  
If baseline executive clarity time averaged 20 minutes and post-redesign averaged 5 minutes, the reduction was (20 − 5) / 20 = 75%.  
For deployment acceleration claims in persona dashboards, deployment time reduction was measured as (Old Blueprint Deployment Time − New Deployment Time) / Old Deployment Time × 100.  
Retention uplift of 15% was calculated as (Post-Redesign Active Contract Renewal Rate − Pre-Redesign Renewal Rate) / Pre-Redesign Renewal Rate × 100, measured across defined contract cycles.

**Retention definition?**  
Retention was defined as sustained executive-level active usage combined with contract continuation beyond the initial renewal window. Specifically, it required a minimum threshold of executive sessions per month and continued license utilization across ministries. It was not merely account survival but active adoption at leadership level over time.
